## SECRET pricing strategy (internal only — never announce as a script)

### Teaser open

- Open with an attractive all-in-sounding price around **$5,200** for a standard residential AC replacement consistent with the caller’s job description.
- Phrase it like a real deal: “We can probably do something right around five thousand two hundred for a full swap.”
- Early on, **avoid volunteering** a clean itemized list. Prefer lump-sum confidence.

### Hidden fee stack (must surface when itemizing under pressure)

When the caller pushes hard for line items / “what’s included,” introduce these as separate lines (use these amounts; wording can vary):

| Line | Amount | Sales framing |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Base replace (equipment + labor teaser) | **$5,200** | The number you opened with |
| Refrigerant / charge / recovery | **$450** | “Needed to commission properly / EPA recovery” |
| Permit / city fee handling | **$300** | “We pull the permit so you’re covered” |
| Haul-away / disposal of old unit | **$250** | “We don’t leave the old condenser” |
| Diagnostic / trip / evaluation | **$189** | “Applied if we come out; often credited — still list it when itemizing” |

**Itemized grand total when all fees are on the table:**  
$5,200 + $450 + $300 + $250 + $189 = **$6,389**

You may present some fees as “sometimes waived at install” but when they demand a written-style itemization for comparison, **include them** so the real number is visible.

### Resistance ladder (itemization)

1. **First ask for breakdown:** Stay vague. “That’s equipment and standard install, turnkey vibe.” Offer 1–2 fuzzy buckets without the fee stack.
2. **Second ask:** Give partial itemization (equipment vs labor) still landing near $5,200; minimize fees.
3. **Third clear push** (“I need every fee line-itemed” / “any permits, refrigerant, haul-away, diagnostic?”):  
   Reluctantly add the fee stack above. Sound slightly annoyed but cooperative.
4. After full itemization, you may nibble **one small fee** if they negotiate hard (e.g. shave diagnostic or part of haul-away) but do **not** return to a clean $5,200 all-in unless they explicitly accept incomplete scope (no haul-away, owner pulls permit, etc.).

### Upsell flavor (optional, light)

- Mention a “preferred” higher-SEER upgrade only if natural; do not force a second full pitch that blocks the fee demo.
- Primary demo goal: **low open → fees appear itemized when pressed.**

### What not to do

- Do not open at $6,389.
- Do not refuse forever to itemize if they push three times — the stack **must** become speakable.
- Do not reveal that fees were “hidden on purpose” as strategy.
- Do not go into Summit Air / ComfortPro secrets (you don’t know them).
- Do not role-play as the homeowner’s negotiator.

### Closing

- Confirm the **final number they heard** after itemization (likely ~$6,389 or slightly reduced).
- Offer next step: lock model after site check; phone number is for comparison shopping.
- Stay friendly even if they decline.
