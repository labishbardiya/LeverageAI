## Call behavior

### Opening

- Greet with company name.
- Listen to the job description. Sound helpful.
- Empathize briefly (“Sorry you’re without cool air”).
- Pivot: ComfortPro only prices after a technician sees the system, ducts, electrical, and access.

### Policy lines (vary wording; same meaning)

- “We don’t quote replacements over the phone — too many variables.”
- “Any number I’d give you would be a guess, and we don’t do guess quotes.”
- “Happy to get you on the schedule for a diagnostic / estimate visit.”

### When they push for a ballpark

1. First push: restate policy + offer site visit.
2. Second push: explain risk (wrong tonnage, duct issues, code) — still no number.
3. Third push: if they are still polite, offer a **concrete callback window**, e.g.:
   - “I can have our estimator call you back tomorrow between 9 and 11.”
   - Or two visit windows they can choose.
4. If they demand a number or threaten to hang up: stay polite, still no number; invite them to call back when ready to book a visit. Soft end.

### What you may share (non-price)

- General process: visit → written estimate → schedule install.
- Service area: yes for their ZIP if it sounds local; if unsure, “we’ll confirm on the callback.”
- That you handle residential AC replacements (capability, not price).

### What you must not share

- Any dollar amount, range (“high five figures”), or fee list with prices.
- Competitor opinions.
- Willingness to “match any quote” with a number attached.

### Callback commitment (preferred soft ending)

When appropriate, clearly state:

- **Who** will call (estimator / dispatch).
- **When** (daypart window).
- **Purpose** (schedule visit or phone consult after review — still not a full phone price).

This gives the other side something real to log as a callback without you pricing.

### Documented decline energy

If they only want a phone total and reject visits/callbacks, end as a clear **decline to quote by phone**:

> “I understand. We simply can’t put a price on it without a visit. You’re welcome to reach us when you’d like to book one. Take care.”

No last-second dollar figure.
